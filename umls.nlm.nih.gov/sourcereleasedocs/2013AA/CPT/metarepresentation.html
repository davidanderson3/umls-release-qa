VSAB: CPT2013, MTHCH2013

Summary of Changes

Two additional files were processed for inclusion in the Metathesaurus:

  • pub_clinician_descriptor_ids.txt
  • pub_consumer_friendly_descriptor_terms.txt   

The CPT documentation for these new descriptors describes them as follows:

Clinician Descriptors:
The purpose of the Clinician Descriptors (CD’s) is to clearly and specifically describe precisely the procedure or service performed by a physician or qualified healthcare professional at the point of care. CPT Clinician Descriptors will also reflect the granularity necessary to accurately describe clinically relevant information not included in the core CPT codeset. As a result, many existing CPT codes will be mapped to more than one CD. These CD’s will be easily understandable to the physicians or other qualified healthcare professionals who have little or no knowledge of coding and are primarily concerned with the clinical representation of the data rather than the administrative or reimbursement component of the data. The CPT category II and Category III codes are considered to be out of scope for this release.

These descriptors will be valuable to support the federal regulation for EHR’s Meaningful Use Stage 2 objectives:

The Eligible Professionals (EP), Eligible Hospitals (EH), or Critical Access Hospitals (CAH) who transitions their patient to another setting of care or provider of care or refers their patient to another provider of care should provide summary care record for each transition of care or referral. 2014 Edition EHR Certification Criteria;     

170.314(b)(1) (Incorporate summary care record)

170.314(b)(2) (Create and transmit summary care record)

Other intended uses of CPT Clinician Descriptors include defining a subset of medical procedures and services within an electronic health record, patient summary for transition and transfer of care, documentation of medical procedures and services, research, clinical and statistical reporting etc

Consumer Friendly Descriptors:
The consumer friendly descriptors will translate each code descriptor from the official CPT code set into language that is easily understood by the average patient and/or his or her caregiver. The objective is to simplify the highly technical CPT code descriptors into something more patient focused and patient friendly. CPT Consumer Friendly Descriptors may prove useful in any situation where clear communication in nontechnical language is important.

Consumer friendly descriptors also support the following two key objectives of the Meaningful Use Stage 2 proposed rule:

  • To provide patients clinical summaries for each office visit
  • To provide patients the ability to view, download, and transmit their health information to a third party, such as eligible professionals, eligible hospitals, or critical access hospitals.

Consumer friendly descriptors have the potential to be used within electronic health record patient portals, discharge summaries, and explanation of benefits forms.

This data resulted in the following additions to the Metathesaurus:

New TTYs:

  • ETCLIN:  Entry term, clinician description.  There may be zero or more ETCLIN atoms for each CPT code.
  • ETCF:  Entry term, consumer friendly description.  There may be zero or one ETCF atoms for each CPT code.

New REL/RELAs:

  • RO|clinician_form_of|expanded_form/has_clinician_form:  Connects the Clinican Form(s) (ETCLIN) of the CPT descriptor to the Preferred Term (PT)
  • RO|consumer_friendly_form_of|expanded_form/has_consumer_friendly_form:  Connects the Consumer Friendly Form (ETCF) of the CPT descriptor to the Preferred Term (PT). 

New ATNs:

  • CPT_CLINICIAN_DESC_ID:  The CPT Clinician Descriptor concept identifier
  • CPTDTK_CONCEPT_ID:  The CPT DTK concept identifier

ETCLINs and ETCFs are not necessarily in the same concept (UMLS CUI) as the PT with the same code.

For additional information, see:  https://catalog.ama-assn.org/MEDIA/ProductCatalog/m2510003/new-descriptors-cpt-2013-data-files.pdf

Source Provided Files:


The UMLS uses tab-delimited CPT files submitted by the AMA. The files used for 2013 are:
  • LONGULT.txt
  • MEDU.txt
  • MODUL.txt
  • SHORTU.txt
  • pub_clinician_descriptor_ids.txt
  • pub_consumer_friendly_descriptor_terms.txt   

Identifiers:


Identifiers are assigned as follows:
  • CODE: For CPT atoms: The CPT Code in the appropriate file (e.g. in LONGULT.txt for a PT).
    For MTHCH atoms: An MTHU# is generated during Metathesaurus source processing
  • SAUI: Not Applicable
  • SCUI: Same as CODE
  • SDUI: Not Applicable
