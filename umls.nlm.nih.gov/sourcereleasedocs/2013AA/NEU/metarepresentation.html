VSAB: NEU2012_08_23

Summary of Changes

1. New permanent numeric identifier called brainInfoID. IDs used prior to 2009 are mapped to ATN = CNID.

2. Synonyms are available in several languages. They are assigned specific termtypes (TTY):

  • CHISY (Chinese)
  • FRESY (French)
  • GERSY (German)
  • INDSY (Indonesian)
  • ITASY (Italian)
  • KORSY (Korean)
  • LATSY (Latin)
  • RUSSY (Russian)
  • SPASY (Spanish)
  • TURSY (Turkish)

3. Latin atoms were previously assigned TTY=LSY (in NEU99). It is now LATSY to remain consistent with the 3-letter language format.

4. Other new TTYs: ACR

5. New ATNs:

  • CNIDTYPE - identifies whether an item is in the classical hierarchy of brain structures (value = h) or not (value = a for ancillary).
  • CNID - IDs used prior to 2009.
  • URL - URL of the central directory page in BrainInfo.

6. New species restrictions: guenon, rodent, mouse, primate, rat. Assigned to ATN = NSR (NeuroNames species restriction).

7. New relationship: SFO/LFO|expanded_form_of between the PT and ACR.
