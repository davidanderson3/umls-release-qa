Notes:
In addition to the LNC SAB, LOINC includes nine subsources, which are assigned by an algorithm using field 34 (EXTERNAL_COPYRIGHT_NOTICE), field 9 (CLASS) and field 7 (METHOD_TYP). The representation described below apply to elements from all subsources. Due to licensing restrictions, concepts from subsources TIMP (Test of Infant Motor Performance) and PROMIS (Patient Reported Outcomes Measurement System survey) are not included in Metathesaurus source processing.

Summary of Changes:

Changes since LNC236

Significant changes have been made to the LOINC data format, including new subsources, new fields and deleted fields.  In addition, changes have been made in Metathesaurus source processing to make LOINC more consistent with the way that other sources are processed.

Original Files:

The UMLS uses files publically available at www.loinc.org, including: "LOINCDB.TXT" (compressed in "LOINC238.zip") and "LOINC_V238_MULTI-AXIAL_HIERARCHY.CSV" (compressed in "LOINC_V238_MULTI-AXIAL_HIERARCHY.ZIP"). In addition, the UMLS receives two files to facilitate building the hierarchy. For 2.38, these files were: "LOINC_PART_LINKS_V2-38_2011-12-20.txt" and "LOINC_PARTS_V2-38_2011-12-20.txt" Additional information, such as expansions of abbreviations, is extracted from LOINCManual.pdf.

Overview

Identifiers

LOINC Identifiers in the UMLS are generally based on field 1 of LOINCDB.TXT (LOINC_NUM), or field 1 of LOINC_PARTS (L_PART_ID). For some termtypes, the atom may be associated with multiple LOINC entities. For example, a component may be a part of multiple fully-specified names. In these cases, the atom (e.g. a CN, or Official Component Name), is assigned an MTHU# during Metathesaurus source processing.

  • CODE:
    • For term types LC, LN, LO, MTH_LN, MTH_LO, OLC, OOSN, OSN: the LOINC _NUM (field 1) from LOINCDB.TXT
    • For term types LPDN, LPN: the L_PART_ID (field 1) from LOINC_PARTS_*.txt
    • For term types CN, MTH_CN, HC, HS, LS: an MTHU#, assigned during Metathesaurus source processing.
  • SAUI: Not Applicable
  • SCUI: Same as CODE for term types LC, LN, LO, LPDN, LPN, MTH_LN, MTH_LO, OLC, OOSN, OSN
  • SDUI: Not Applicable

Expansions

Expanded forms of certain atoms are created during Metathesaurus source processing by applying abbreviation expansions extracted from the LOINC Manual. In these cases, the expanded form is generally assigned the same identifier as its original form. Expansions may also include converting patterns such as "little e" or “super little b" to “e" or "^b" respectively.
