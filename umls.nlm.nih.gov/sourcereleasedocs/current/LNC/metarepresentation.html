Notes:
In addition to the LNC SAB, LOINC includes 16 subsources, which are assigned by an algorithm using field 33 (EXTERNAL_COPYRIGHT_NOTICE). The representation described below apply to elements from all subsources. Due to licensing restrictions, concepts from the following subsources are not included in Metathesaurus processing:

  • BOMC (Blessed Orientation-Memory-Concentration Test)
  • BSL (Borderline Symptom List)
  • Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale
  • NSRAS (Neonatal Skin Risk Assessment Scale)
  • TIMP (Test of Infant Motor Performance)
  • Portions (by Barbara Huffines)
  • PROMIS (Patient Reported Outcomes Measurement System survey)

Summary of Changes:

1. New termgroups:
     LNC244_FACIT/LO
     LNC244_FACIT/MTH_LO
     LNC244_FACIT/OLC

2. New relationship attributes (RELA) and inverses:
     has_quotient | quotient_of
     has_multipart | multipart_of

3. Deprecated termgroups:
      MTH_LPN

4. Deprecated relationship attributes (RELA) and inverses:
     mth_plain_text_form_of | mth_has_plain_text_form

5. Deprecated attributes:
     MTH_COPYRIGHT
     MTH_LEA
     MTH_LFO
     MTH_LQT
     MTH_LRN2
     MTH_LSU
     MAPSETTYPE
     MTH_SOS

6. Deprecated subsources:
     AD8_2005
     MDS30

7. The process for identifying subsources has changed. Subsources are created based on the external copyright field (field 33) only. If the copyright field is blank or ambiguous the SAB=LNC. Due to this new process two subsources have been deprecated, AD8_2005 and MDS30.

8. Entries in the LOINC_PARTS file that do not have an entry in the LOINC_PART_LINKS file or the LOINC_*_MULTI-AXIAL_HIERARCHY file are considered orphans and are not processed.

9. Entries in the LOINC_PARTS file that have a PART_TYPE of 'QUOTIENTS' and 'MULTIPART' are now included as part of the inversion. In previous inversions these were ignored. New RELAs and corresponding inverses were created.

10. Metathesaurus-created ATNs, RELs, and TTYs (e.g., MTH_*) that were created as a result of plain text versions of UTF-8 strings have been deprecated because MetamorphoSys (MMSY) with its enhanced indexes can now process UTF-8 characters.

11. MAP_TO.txt file replaces the retired MAP_TO field in the LOINCDB.TXT file. (The MAP_TO field was deprecated in LNC242). Subsequently, ATN=LMP which was also deprecated has been resurrected.

12. ATN=MAPSETTYPE will no longer be created unless the information is provided by the source.

13. TTYs LN/LC/CN rank higher than their corresponding MTH_* termtype. This applies to LNC and all subsources.

14. Attributes now use the LNC code as the ID rather than the SRC_ATOM_ID. The id_type (CODE_SOURCE) and id_qualifier (LNC244) have been updated to reflect this.


Original Files:

The UMLS uses files publically available at www.loinc.org, including: "LOINCDB.TXT" (compressed in "LOINC244.zip") and "LOINC_V244_MULTI-AXIAL_HIERARCHY.CSV" (compressed in "LOINC_V244_MULTI-AXIAL_HIERARCHY.ZIP"). In addition, the UMLS receives two files to facilitate building the hierarchy. For 2.44, these files were: "LOINC_PART_LINKS_V2-44_2013-06-20.txt" and "LOINC_PARTS_V2-44_2013-06-20.txt". Additional information, such as expansions of abbreviations, is extracted from LOINCManual.pdf.

Overview

Identifiers

LOINC Identifiers in the UMLS are generally based on field 1 of LOINCDB.TXT (LOINC_NUM), or field 1 of LOINC_PARTS (L_PART_ID). For some termtypes, the atom may be associated with multiple LOINC entities. For example, a component may be a part of multiple fully-specified names. In these cases, the atom (e.g. a CN, or Official Component Name), is assigned an MTHU# during Metathesaurus source processing.

  • CODE:
    • For term types LC, LN, LO, MTH_LN, MTH_LO, OLC, OOSN, OSN: the LOINC _NUM (field 1) from LOINCDB.TXT
    • For term types LPDN, LPN: the L_PART_ID (field 1) from LOINC_PARTS_*.txt
    • For term types CN, MTH_CN, HC, HS, LS: an MTHU#, assigned during Metathesaurus source processing.
  • SAUI: Not Applicable
  • SCUI: Same as CODE for term types LC, LN, LO, LPDN, LPN, MTH_LN, MTH_LO, OLC, OOSN, OSN
  • SDUI: Not Applicable

Expansions

Expanded forms of certain atoms are created during Metathesaurus source processing by applying abbreviation expansions extracted from the LOINC Manual. In these cases, the expanded form is generally assigned the same identifier as its original form. Expansions may also include converting patterns such as "little e" or “super little b" to “e" or "^b" respectively.
